World Communicable Diseases 2018

Visualisation by: Chu Lee Shen

A communicable disease is one that passes between people or animals. IT is also known as "infectious" or "transmissible" diseases. Malaria is a serious and sometimes fatal disease caused by a parasite that commonly infects a certain type of mosquito which feeds on humans. 'Neglected tropical diseases' (NTDs) is an umbrella term to describe a group of communicable diseases which causes immesurerable suffering over 1.7 billion people. It is extremely common in tropical areas where people do not have access to clean water or safe ways to dispose of human waste.
