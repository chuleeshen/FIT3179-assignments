World Communicable Diseases

Communicable disease statistics of the world (countries recognized by WHO) updated until year 2020

A communicable disease is one that passes between people or animals. It is also known as "infectious" or "transmissible" diseases. Communicable diseases can be prevented and the three deadliest communicable diseases are malaria, tuberculosis and HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus).

Malaria is a serious and sometimes fatal disease caused by a parasite that commonly infects a certain type of mosquito which feeds on humans. 'Neglected tropical diseases' (NTDs) is an umbrella term to describe a group of communicable diseases which causes immesurerable suffering over 1.7 billion people. It is extremely common in tropical areas where people do not have access to clean water or safe ways to dispose of human waste.
